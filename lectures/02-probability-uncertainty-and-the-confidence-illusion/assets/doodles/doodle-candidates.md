# Doodle candidates — Chapter 02 (scout pass)

Progressive-disclosure sketch moments mined from the chapter. Candidates only — the
slide-deck skill decides which become Doodle specs. One idea per doodle; draw as it's
narrated.

- **The 99%-accurate test that kills the patient** — draw one patient, a big "99%
  ACCURATE" stamp, then reveal a crowd of healthy people behind them; most of the
  positive flags land on the healthy crowd. (Pairs with base-rate-icon-array.)
- **Bayes as bookkeeping** — prior box → arrow (the evidence) → posterior box; the
  arrow only *nudges*, it doesn't overwrite. Shows updating, not replacing.
- **Confidence that doesn't move when the world does** — a needle gauge pinned at
  0.9 while the ground underneath it shifts (distribution shift); the model stays
  sure while being wrong.
- **Conditional probability narrows the world** — full sample space, then "given B"
  shrinks the frame to just B; the probability is re-normalized inside the smaller box.
- **Heavy tails vs the friendly bell** — a tidy bell curve, then one far-out point
  drags the running average; "the next observation can still move your mean."
- **The multiplication rule as a path** — a branching tree; the joint probability is
  the product along one path (cards-without-replacement scene).

Notes: the first three are the strongest teaching moments; the base-rate one overlaps
the icon-array figure, so use one or the other per slide, not both.
