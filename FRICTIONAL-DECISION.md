# Deciding the Frictional apparatus home (Editorial issue #1)

The editorial review's highest-leverage fix: the cut "Frictional Method" chapter left its apparatus — the prediction-lock journal, the AI Use Disclosure, the case template — orphaned, referenced by ~nine chapters' cross-refs, Ch9's deliverables, and Ch13's final-memo assembly. This note settles where that apparatus lives, using the Frictional research it came from.

## What the Frictional idea actually is

The "Frictional Method" is not a loose device this book invented. It is one application of the **Frictional framework** (Nik Bear Brown, Irreducibly Human / Frictional.xyz), argued in full in the preprint *Measuring the Struggle* and expanded into its own textbook, `friction-measuring-the-learning-struggle` (summary: that repo's `PAPER-SUMMARY.md`).

The core Frictional claim: generative AI decoupled the artifact from the process that used to produce it, so genuine cognitive engagement must be evidenced by its **friction traces** — the behavioral residue of real learning — rather than by the polished output. In the education setting that becomes the seven-component GLP score. In *this* book's setting, the "Frictional Method" chapter was the same idea aimed at validation work: prediction-lock before you observe, keep the journal, disclose AI use — the friction traces of a supervisor who actually did the epistemic work rather than laundering a fluent AI artifact.

So it is relevant, not deletable. The prediction-lock/journal/Disclosure apparatus is the friction-trace idea operationalized for the skeptic. That is exactly why so many chapters lean on it, and exactly why cutting the chapter without re-homing the apparatus broke them.

## The decision

**Do not reinstate a Frictional Method chapter.** It belongs to its own book. Instead, re-home the apparatus as a *named, credited primitive* and point outward for the theory:

1. **Prediction-lock discipline → Chapter 1.** It is a skeptic's-toolkit move (commit the falsification condition before you see the output — it's Popper's instrument already living in Ch1), and the whole casebook depends on it from Ch2 onward. Introduce it once in Ch1's toolkit, name it "prediction-lock," and let every later `[verify-xref: Frictional Method chapter cut]` resolve to "Chapter 1's prediction-lock."

2. **The casebook journal → Chapter 1's "Meet the casebook" / Pebble contract.** The running journal on the student's own agent is the Pebble thread; its setup belongs where the Pebble is introduced, not in a separate chapter.

3. **AI Use Disclosure → Chapter 9 (Delegation).** It's a delegation/accountability deliverable; it fits the handoff-contract material and Ch9 already extends it. Move the definition there; earlier mentions become forward pointers.

4. **One credited sentence, once** (Ch1 or the author's note): note that the prediction-lock/journal discipline is the Frictional framework applied to validation, and cite the Frictional work for readers who want the neurobiological case. This keeps the book honest about provenance without importing a whole chapter of learning-science.

## Execution once approved

- Global fix: replace every `[verify-xref: Frictional Method chapter cut]` with the correct new pointer per the map above (mechanical after the four homes are set).
- Ch9: add the AI Use Disclosure definition; Ch1: add the prediction-lock primitive + the one-sentence Frictional credit.
- Re-run the casebook-exercise regeneration (editorial issue #2) against these homes, so the LLM-exercise "Connection/Preview" blocks stop referencing a dead chapter.

Net: the Frictional idea stays credited and load-bearing, the orphaned refs get real homes, and the book doesn't carry a learning-science chapter that belongs to a different book.
