# Research Notes: Chapter 11 — Visualization Under Validation: Honest, Misleading, and the Choices Between
**Corresponding chapter:** chapters/11-visualization-under-validation-honest-misleading-and-the-choices-between.md · **Editor note:** notes/11-visualization-under-validation-honest-misleading-and-the-choices-between.md · **Generated:** 2026-07-01

## Chapter summary
The chapter argues that a dashboard's structural choices constitute an argument about what the data means, made before any number is read — so a misleading visualization of valid findings is a validation failure at the output layer and the supervisor's responsibility. It builds honest vs. misleading dashboards from identical data, catalogs deceptive moves, and anchors them in a perception hierarchy (Cleveland-McGill) plus four historical cases (Challenger, Catalonia, Reuters/Florida, Snow) and a proxy-failure case (GDELT/Nigeria).

- **Claim:** Presenting mammography screening as natural frequencies (vs. conditional probabilities) dramatically improves Bayesian reasoning; ~95/100 physicians overestimate post-test probability when given conditional probabilities. · **Source:** Gigerenzer et al., "Helping Doctors and Patients Make Sense of Health Statistics," *Psychological Science in the Public Interest* 8(2), 2007, pp. 53–96 (PDF https://www.stat.berkeley.edu/~aldous/157/Papers/health_stats.pdf); Gigerenzer, "What are natural frequencies?" *BMJ* 2011 (https://pure.mpg.de/rest/items/item_2099208_9/component/file_3562683/content) · primary · **Verdict:** CONFIRMED — canonical numbers: base rate ~0.8%, 90% sensitivity, 7% false-positive → posterior ~7.8% (≈8/103); natural-frequency framing raises correct answers substantially. - **Claim:** Tufte's "lie factor" (size of effect in graphic ÷ size of effect in data; 1.0 = honest) and "data-ink ratio" as integrity/efficiency measures. · **Source:** Tufte, E.R., *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information*, Graphics Press, 1983 (2nd ed. 2001) · primary · **Verdict:** CONFIRMED — lie factor defined; Tufte flags |lie factor| outside ~0.95–1.05 as substantial distortion; data-ink ratio = data-ink / total ink.

- **Claim:** The Reuters/Florida "Stand Your Ground" gun-deaths chart misled via an inverted y-axis. · **Source:** Chart by Christine Chan, Reuters, Feb 2014; documented in Bergstrom & West, *Calling Bullshit* (2020) and callingbullshit.org lecture 6; LiveScience (https://www.livescience.com/45083-misleading-gun-death-chart.html), Pacific Standard (https://psmag.com/news/mislead-charts-stand-ground-laws-gun-deaths-79726/) · secondary · **Verdict:** CONFIRMED — the y-axis was inverted (0 at top, 800 at bottom), making a post-2005 rise read as a fall; deaths jumped 521 (2005) → 740 (2006) → 825 (2007).
