# Computational Skepticism for AI
## Compact Tik TOC Architecture

**Book type:** Course textbook / field-defining practitioner text  
**Status:** Retrospective architecture for a developed manuscript  

## Core Thesis

This book teaches readers to evaluate AI outputs, claims, charts, code, and arguments with disciplined doubt rather than reflexive trust or reflexive rejection. The central capability is computational skepticism: asking what would make an output wrong, what evidence supports it, what measurement hides, and what verification would actually change confidence.

## Primary Reader

A student, researcher, analyst, educator, or professional who uses AI systems but needs a durable method for checking plausibility, evidence, uncertainty, and output integrity.

## Learning Arc

1. **Skeptical foundations:** Cartesian doubt, Humean limits, Popperian falsifiability, and Plato's Cave as practical AI-output tests.
2. **Applied audits:** text, data, statistics, images, code, summaries, citations, and workflows.
3. **Operational skepticism:** repeatable review protocols, documentation, team practice, and high-stakes refusal.

## Chapter Architecture

Each chapter should begin with a convincing AI output that is not yet trustworthy. The reader then applies one skeptical move, runs a verification protocol, and documents what changed.

## Terminal Capability

The reader can audit an AI-assisted artifact, name its failure modes, design checks that would catch those failures, and decide whether to trust, revise, reject, or escalate the output.

## Adoption Risks

- The book can sound anti-AI if the constructive workflow is not visible.
- Philosophy can feel abstract unless every concept lands in a practical audit.
- Fast-changing AI products should not replace the stable skeptical method.

## Production Notes

Use Tik TOC to make the skeptical moves spiral: each return should add a new artifact type or risk level, not repeat the same warning.
